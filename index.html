Ilish Thomas,
Echo (a).

The practice I aim to apply to the presentation strategy of video work online, is the current audio compositions and sound sculptures I’m working with in my studio practice [1]. Recently I’ve been investigating the effects of sonic forms and the ability to traverse people and communities over time through sound and music. Taking from a singular vocal audio sample, an aural assertion, a signifying tone exclaimed by the performer of a track (with which I selected) to begin the journey of this project.

Using movement, rhythm, repetition and reproduction and modes within the process to develop and snowball the work in this years’ studio practice. I subsequently built an altar-like sculpture, a speaker atop a tall plinth, that was then installed in the dark in 6B15 (the tv studio) lit by a single, soft spotlight. The intention was to use formal aspects of the structure of the plinth and speaker to generate and perform as a sculptural ‘body’ of/for sound.

It performed and acted as a sort of repurposed sound beacon with which the audience quietly and patiently engaged with as the sound looped [2].
